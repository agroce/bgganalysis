RATING: 7
This game has been described as a game for A&A players looking for something a little more complex with more historical flavor without being overly burdening. This game fits that bill. The gameplay is thoroughly engaging and the way units are researched and built adds a unique layer of strategy to the game from the very beginning as far as how you decide to build up your nation's forces. Do you want to try and make Germany a naval power in this game and contest the Allies in the Atlantic and the Med? You can do that in this game! The game will still play long, likely anywhere between 8-12 hrs for a playthrough. The game has an excellent historical flavor from the use of real army command-level units (i.e. German Panzer Army Afrika, US 18th Airborne Corps, etc). The only reason I'm giving it a 7 is because the component quality is questionable and there's not a lot here despite its hefty price tag. The map is cheaply made and incredibly too small for all the counters that come with the game, especially the larger counters. The rulebook is disorganized and very wordy, which significantly contributes to its 40+ pages. Country setup sheets are included but there are no player aid sheets specifically just for commonly used rules. Instead the designer has added common rules to the map itself in the form of tables and text boxes, but in a very unorganized and seemingly random manner that overly clutters the map. If a 2nd edition was released that fixed these issues, I would easily rate this game a 9.

RATING: 6
First, other than some of the graphics, this is NOT WORLD IN FLAMES. This is super-advanced Axis and Allies (or whatever). It is a somewhat fun, somewhat playable global wargame.

PRO:
- a pre-war start option is nice - to change the set up, however it heavily favors the allies. (i.e., if you do all that happened, the Axis would come out BEHIND what historically happened)

- Nice addition of force pool units allow for the expansion of units and types as the war progresses.

- the idea of making each turn shorter, so that later in the war as the belligerents geared up more is quite clever.


CONS:

- Price. Wow, it is expensive for how little you get (small unmounted map, too few players aid cards, and a couple of counter sheets.). Should be about $50, not $84!!!!!

- Map small, hard to read, and not attractive. (advertised as 'large'? when did a 22x34 inch map become large? MOST games this is standard!!!! and for a GLOBAL game, this is tiny!). Another truly unfortunate decision was the division of the map. For whatever reason the map is divided not in the middle of the US, but the pacific. Thus tha Pacific war is carried out on both ends of the map, rather than one side. Makes it hard to follow tha game.

- Play aids are incomplete and not helpful.

- Rules are missing in areas - and need better indexing and a proper index in the back. (for example, land area combat rule 5.3 goes on for three pages without one sub paragraph identifier).
