RATING: 6
I was pleasantly surprised by this game considering  I have a track record of disliking well regarded pnps.

Positives:
- Didn't even need to print it. Just played the pdf as is with a word doc keeping track of my stats.
- Dice resource management and timing feel important, e.g. there were several times I had to spend food to reroll dice since I was down to 1 or 2 active dice, but my remaining dice were high numbers. Ideally, I should have spent them before having to reroll. Also decisions on whether to use a higher die to take better treasures or take less damage from a monster, or save it for a potentially more dire situation.
- Character progression

Negatives:
- There are still a few times where decisions feel fairly obvious, although considering the length and simplicity not too much of a problem.
- The rulebook has some redundancy that by over repeating things makes it less clear, since I already got it the first time and the repetition makes me question if I did understand it correctly. Most noticeably this occurs with having to lose a health to reroll a die if you are out of dice and food. Also I think it is more intuitive to put attack icons before defense icons.
