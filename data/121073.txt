The rules are very easy, you can explain them under 3 minutes, but the gameplay is rather hard. Our second game took a long time because every time we made a mistake we returned a VP, so at the end we played without this rule so the game could end. The game´s art is ok.

As is usual from thins kind go game, when a player has more experience with the game, he will destroy all others, so it is best when played with players of similar skill. 

Bottom line, a fun party game. My GF likes it a lot so I will buy it. I have played it 3 times and I’m getting better at it.

I love speed games. I'm good at speed games. But this one - nobody wants to play it with me. They're all scared. Everybody! And yet a 6 y.o. kid beat me fair and square. 

Panic Lab is a speed deduction game, but unlike Geistesblitz where it's possible to internalise the rules and immediately know the right answer, Panic Lab is deduction done step by step. It's actually quite a stress for my mind - [i]you have to follow procedure, first A, then B, then aha, aha, aha, and you have to keep all these steps in memory as you go along.[/i] And do it fast. Aaa! Any you can't really on your skills or your knowledge of the game as you're always back on square one. 

What's going on is that you have these amoebas lined up in a circle - there's two of each (they come in 2 shapes, 2 colours, 2 patterns) and you roll the dice and that one it shows you have to find. Starting at the proper lab tile and going in the proper direction, oh, and when you pass an air vent, you skip tiles, and come out at the next air vent, and when you enter a mutation chamber, you're searching a changed amoebae. Clear? 

I want to like this game, but - it is tough and nobody wants to play it with me! It's suitable for as a one off, as I don't think you can gain a lot of skill in it (little internalisation), you just have to get familiar with it and don't give up. So - if you have a logic freak in your family this will work, if you have kids willing to have fun with this, it will teach deductive skills. If they think it's fun. 

I had this conversation with a 12 y.o. girl I tried to teach it to:
[i]"This game is stupid! It's not logical!"
"Au contraire young lady, this game is 100% nothing but logical." [/i]

This is a speed pattern-recognition follow-the-path game along the lines of Bongo, but different. There are 3 colored lab cards, mixed in amongst a bunch of other cards, all in a big circle. The cards show either:
- amoebas with one/two eyes, with strips/spots, in one of the three colors
- a vent (3 in game)
- a transmogrifier: one for eye number, one for skin pattern, and one for each pair of colors

You roll 4 dice:
- lab die, indicating which lab the amoeba is escaping from, and which direction it runs
- dice for eye number, skin pattern, and color

Players simultaneously determine which amoeba they are looking for, then follow the path from the lab out wards. If they hit a vent, they pop out the next vent in the circle in that direction. If they hit a transmogrifier, the target amoeba changes accordingly. When you think you've found the right target, put your finger on it. Wait till everyone has a target. Whoever was the first person to find the correct target scores a VP. Play till one player wins a certain (4?) number of VP.

Fun and pretty challenging game (for me, at least), but not one I'm likely to play too often!
