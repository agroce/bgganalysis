RATING: N/A
Of the three sets I currently own (I plan on buying the Fantasia set eventually, though I don't own it at the time of this writing) this is my least favorite, and coincidentally it's my son's least favorite as well. The images are all intended to be actions, but the die faces show an actor and an acted-upon, and when my son uses this set, he focuses more on the nouns than the verb that is being abstracted. That's not an awful thing -- there's no standard by which your play needs to be measured with these dice -- but of the three sets, this is the one that becomes the most restrictive in practice for him. One day we'll get out of that rut, but for now, these are not as good as the original set or the Voyages set for him. 

I've used this set (and others) with my son mainly as bedtime story generators. We mix and match sets, we tell a story on our own, we alternate every-other-die, and we have even had stories where the dice get to interact with one another by moving them around the play surface.
