Abstract:
A sci-fi, semi-coop game adapting the Alien movies. It has gorgeous looking minis! Nemesis makes use of hidden objectives similar to Dead of Winter: A Crossroads Game which may turn into fun player interaction like secret objective "player number 3 must die" but you are not allowed to directly attack other players, so you have to sabotage them somehow. The combat is interesting, because hit points are randomly assigned to an Alien after each turn, so you never know how much hit points it has left. 

Pros:
+ Fun combat due to variable Alien life
+ Alien theme
+ Good artwork and high quality minis
+ Survival atmosphere (low ammunition, risky movement, push-your-luck dice rolls)

Cons:
- Characters do not play very differently
- Most of the time the game feels like pick up and delivery
- Many turns are not exciting: do healing, do repairing, walk to room to recheck what another players has done
- ...sometimes it is even better to do nothing in your turn
- It is often too risky to move because you likely may encounter an Alien
- Some secret goals are too easy to guess after you know which goals exist in the game
- Not much cooperation or interaction between players in semi-coop mode (depending on secret goals)
- Very few opportunities to meditate dice rolls
- Rotatable rooms are a neat idea but does not add much to the game experience

Thoughts:
I really like the theme and the general idea. However, I fear Nemesis might lack some depth and might feel repetitive over time. The game forces you to do a lot of uninteresting things like searching, repairing, healing, resupplying, delivering objects to prevent you from going after your secret objective - rewards are very rare in this game.

Similar games:
* Dead of Winter (semi-coop survival game with more variation and player interaction)

Player: 1-5 | Time: 90-180m

!!! Disclaimer : Based on 1 prototype play !!!

Checking out the game beforehand it seemed i might like it a lot. 
"Alien" horror vibe, hidden objectives, hand management. 
So we need engines working to get the ship going, can escape in pods.
Kill aliens, drag theyre corpses to the lab to research them.

The rooms are randomly distributed, there is a variable amount loot in the rooms that can be searched for. When a room catches fire it can even destroy the loot, how cool is that? We can destroy or repair rooms, build weapons change to direction of the ship.

Holy crap, I want this game so bad!
It took me half a game to discover that there is not much variety in each loot deck wich made me less excited about what might come up. 
But far far worse were the dice... they hosed me. If I moved to a 'safe spot' where i could roll anything but 1 number I rolled that number consitently.  I don't think I managed to land a single hit using my gun. 
There is not much to mitigate bad luck. 

My objective : send signal, set desination to mars, either escape using pod or survive in hibernate.
Looking at the cards currently in the "objectives" decks I suggest just to swap for a "alternate" they are probably the easiest to achieve.

 Dice hate me, I can't "survive" games that depend on luck this much while offering not enough ways to mitigate.

Rating based on 1 play of the game at its current state.
