RATING: 7.5
Dojo Kun is a mid-weight worker placement euro game.
So, where is the appeal, you may say...?
There is lot to like, here: the game is quite thematic (and the theme is unique and not dull at all), gameplay is fast and definitely exciting. The twist lies in the build-up to the tournament turns, where all the excitement previously compressed, will unleash like a spring.
The game lasts just 8 turns, and turns 4 and 8 are the tournament turns: all worker placements are put aside, and a single elimination 8-man dice-fest begins!
The player who has trained the best fighter usually wins, but every fighter earns a fistful of victory points for winning each match.

A very fun and fresh design, mixing euro and dice rolling in a very pleasant way. Want to play again!

RATING: 7.5
Very solid worker placement game.

Everything makes thematic sense; only (slight) downside is the unfortunately quite random nature of the tournaments. 

Winning the 4nd phase of each round (the tournament) gives a big VP boost that is hard to overcome in any other way. However, since winning tournaments is heavily influenced by dice rolls, the 3 previous phases feel less important.

Which is not to say that they are not; these define how many dices a fighter can use during the fights in a tournament ("levels"), increase the number of attacks, learn special combos and moves, complete quests for VPs and other stuff, or simply earn VPs by training or promoting the Dojo.

As such, the player has a lot of possibilities of getting VPs and increasing his odds with the dice rolls; just bear in mind that chance is a factor in getting VPs through tournaments.

Long story short, it's a very solid game, very fun and enjoyable.
