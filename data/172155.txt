RATING: 5
*Rating based on iOS app.

After loving Realms Unraveled, I was looking forward to Dawn of Champions (DoC) with much anticipation.  Sadly, I found it very disappointing.

DoC introduces Champions, which are faction-specific role cards that remain separate from your deck.  You can gain reputation by spending runes, buying cards of your faction for your deck, or defeating monsters of your faction (faction-specific monsters are another new addition to this set).  When you accumulate enough reputation, you gain a special hero card into your deck.  Additionally, when you reach 8 reputation, each time you buy/defeat a card of your faction, you can "rally" (see below).

When I read the rules for Champions, it initially sounded very cool.  However, I found them wanting in a few regards.  First, the special hero cards are decent, but they're not as special as I would have liked them to be.  Second, I would have liked to have seen more ways to gain reputation.  Instead of all factions spending runes to gain reputation, I would have liked to see the Void champion spend power, and the Lifebound champion get reputation by playing Unite abilities, and the Mechana champion get reputation by destroying a construct... something that would make playing the different champions feel more unique.  Third, as I will discuss below, I dislike the rally mechanism and would have preferred some other kind of bonus for reaching the maximum reputation threshold.

Speaking of the rally mechanism, that brings me to my other main gripe with the set.  The rally keyword is paired with a faction name (or "monster") and means that when a card is flipped from the main deck to be added to the center row, if it matches the rally type (e.g. Rally: Mechana), you immediately gain/defeat that card for free AND continue rallying to the next card.  This mechanism is entirely random.  It's complete random chance whether the next card off the deck will match the rally faction, and this can create some incredibly unfair chains.  I've seen 4+ cards rallied in a row.  This creates wild swings, which completely unbalances the game.  I hate when the Ascension designers add unnecessary randomness to the game, and I absolutely despise this mechanism.

Many of the individual cards in this set are pretty cool, and I like having the factioned monsters, but the two major mechanisms introduced in this set just fell so flat for me that it ruined the whole experience.  I still play with this set combined with Realms Unraveled, but I am always left thinking of how much better it could have been.

RATING: 8.5
My favorite set so far. The mechanic for this set is that each player gets a champion and a card corresponding to the champion. In order to earn your champion card you must gain reputation in 1 of 2 ways, you can get it by buying cards that match your champions type (mechana, lifebound, void, or enlightened) or by paying 2 runes once a turn.

Champion abilities later on can be very powerful with the rally mechanic that allows you to obtain the card you replace when buying/defeating cards in the center as long as the type matches what the rally type is.

Gives you a good sense of direction when you're unsure of what cards to buy in the center row.

The rally mechanic can lead to some crazy turns if you happen to get lucky enough especially if you've maxed out your champions reputation. This can make the game a bit swingy at times but after having played it many many times overall the better player will still win a higher percentage of the time.
