Early days, so my opinion might change, but so far this is Black Plague +

RATING: 9
As expected, the system is very familiar. The departures, however, are super interesting and nice changes to the formula.

The Hedges: Simple line of sight manipulation which lets you play cat and mouse with the zombies a bit more. They don't block movement, so there's some interesting interactions that can happen. I also like the possibility of, essentially an "Aaaah!" card if you jump over and no one has line of sight on the space you are moving to.

The Waterholes: Pretty scary in many respects. The higher movement cost makes entering them a risky proposition. That they do not slow zombies solidifies them as a great new element.

The Trebuchet: I think this exists solely for the reason that you have to have a tool to deal with big groups of zombies, such as the horde. It also gives an interesting, if action intensive, alternative to dealing with fatties and abominations. 

The HORDE!!: So, I wasn't sure how this would work. First: When it spawns it terrifying to see. Second: You will realize that the horde sucks up mobs, increasing the chances of running out, and causing extra activations. 

Those are just initial thoughts, all based on the core. This may yet become a 10 if the additional content adds the depth that Wulfsburg did for BP.

RATING: 8
Green Horde brings a nice breath of fresh air to Zombicide: Black Plague. We've been playing it standalone which makes the game feel like an actual struggle for survival. Do you use the trebuchet and waste a whole turn? Should you shoot at a tile or the horde? Is it worth the risk to cross a hedge? Are orcs close to an extra activation because a good portion of them are in the horde? There's also the fact that the zombies hit harder, so you need to play more strategically if you want to survive. The game is great and I'm looking forward to more of the expansions.
