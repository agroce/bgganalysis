RATING: 5
The rulebook can kill the game for you. Combat is not well described, the difference between general event cards and scenario events is not that clear. The way raiders move (f.e. in scenario 2) is very free-form so that they group has to play it their own way (hard or easier).

THe designer is very active in the BGG forum sector but the rulebook is really bad laid out.

I really like the game though but you have to keep in mind when cheking this game out that you have to put some work into it (check out forums etc.) to learn the game.

The concept behind is pretty neat. I like the leveling up, I like the exploration aspect of it, I like the way the wider the galaxy becomes the harder the game gets but the more Astartium you get. I like the combat mechanic - even if it is simple.

The scenario 1 and 2 feel very different but my gaming group does not like the game that much - we had situation where one player really fell behind the rest (level-wise).  Maybe it is because of the design. maybe it is because of our choices.

Scenario 2 can be very frustrating if you have not that many movement points to cross asteroid belts. You are effectively out of the game then...

After more plays the big plus of the game is that it is very tactical and plays very quick.

But the rules will Stop you on every first attempt of a scenario.

Gameplaywise it is an 8.

RATING: 8
I played this at a demo at gen con. Had a very good time playing with my fiance and two other attendees. They did a surprisingly good job at capturing the 4x feel in a co-op game (Please note this was with the Updated rule book, made a world of difference!). In order to hit objectives you'll have to expand via exploration, which in turn gives more spawning grounds for the rogue fighters that slow down movement, but grant resources on beating them. These resources can be spent upgrading one of two tracks (which furthers a third track at certain milestones) which enhance abilities relating to fighting, exploring, repairing, and others. Each person has their own ship stats and their own tech trees, as well as their own pool of one shot cards granted by destroying rogues and exploring. These cards can be used on each other with the owners discretion.

This sets up for a game that forces coordination and specialization. You have to make sure someone is bouncing around taking out rogue fighters while amassing enough power to take down bosses and complete objectives. Overall very fun co-op game The makers are a cool lot and very dedicated. 

Something I'll mention, but have left out of my rating since I haven't experienced it yet: the publisher, Vesuvius Media, has additional scenarios on their forums, allow for user submission, and are working on a crossover campaign with another one of their games ("Cosmic Pioneers"). Once I've given it a go I'll update accordingly.

Sci-fi 4x Co-op! Mission driven hex tiles space exloration. Variable player powers, spend crystals to strengthen your armada by leveling command, combat and research tech trees. Dice based combat and exploration. Use command tokens and upgrade powers to mitigate luck. Intuitive play rules. I love the mystery and excitement of the scanning report system. I also like the exploration threat level condition of the encounters. The recurring events is a fun cumulative damage system. I don't like when the missions tell you exactly when the boss encounters happen--probably a balance thing, wish it could be unknown or uncertain for more suspense. Kudos for game play that does a decent job to encourage teamwork instead of everyone running off on their own against a common goal. I wish there were more variability on the tech trees but oh well. We love tile exploring, challenging co-ops that encourage teamwork, rolling dice, and teching-up. Tempted to back this.
