RATING: 7
Really good expansion. Better than a lot of the stuff they've been coming out with lately. I think this may be my favorite behind Awesome Level 9000 and Science Fiction Double Feature. There isn't anything all that new here, but I like how they've slightly tweaked ideas seen before.

The explorers are really similar to the pirates. They move around bases a lot, but they can also scout out future bases, and their best creature can actually spawn a new base to travel to. A neat twist on an already explored idea (No pun intended).

The grannys do a lot of top-decking, as a lot of them have abilities that involve looking at the top card of your deck, and putting it back or on the bottom. Others allow you to play cards from these two positions if they're certain types as extra moves. Neat faction.

The rock stars are...okay. They were my most hyped faction, but they're probably the least original. They combine the idea of putting out a bunch of same-name characters with a grab bag of other abilities, such as moving characters and shuffling people back into your deck. Extremely middle of the road, as far as I'm concerned.

Lastly, the teddy bears are really similar to the kittens from Pretty Pretty Smash Up. They're very cute, but deadly. Much like some factions that have come before, they have a lot of abilities that really screw with their opponents. Some of them can actually extend the game out longer than I'd like, as they can increase the breaking point of bases and things like that. I don't mind these, but they fall into the same category as the Tricksters for me.

So overall an above average set. I think the folks behind this game realize that there isn't much left to explore that's all that innovative, but I do like how they're doing slight variations on existing ideas. Good stuff.
