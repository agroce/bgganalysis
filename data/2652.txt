RATING: 7
Revised thoughts after many years with the game:

Wow, somehow this has been the "write an answer" party game that has lasted with our group. These days, it even gets more play than my once-totally-beloved Why Did the Chicken...? which I did NOT see coming. 

In the right group, I still prefer WDtC? But the problem is that game actually puts a LOT of onus on the players to be funny and creative - under a time limit no less. All it takes is one player who either isn't comfortable with that expectation or simply isn't in the mood for that kind of mental challenge (these types of games are often our closers so "I'm too tired" is a pretty common refrain) to kill that game dead.

I think this game works so well as a party game, then, because it has a little more structure and a lot less pressure - you're coming up with these obscure old sayings that don't really have to mean anything, just [i]sound[/i] like they could, which is a lot easier than writing a punchline to a joke. And you only have to do one. 

And man, this game is worth grabbing just for the proverbs alone. There's a ton of bizarre ones that are just laugh-out-loud hilarious. I've never played a game where the real answer to at least one of them didn't send everyone into hysterics. Kudos to whoever found all these gems. It does limit how frequently you can play the game if you don't want everyone to start remembering the answers, but we've been playing this for something like 10 years and we still play it nearly monthly.

And that's what makes this game leagues ahead of Balderdash/Dictionary. Proverbs just work so much better (and are so much funnier) than obscure words. Though it does share my chief problem with those games, which is that players often have to choose between being funny or playing to win. Outlandish answers can sometimes trick people because, again, the real proverbs can be so bizarre in themselves, but quite often I feel the need to reign it in a bit. And ultimately, I want my party games to be about laughter. This game provides plenty of it, but sometimes the design feels at odds.

On that note, the score track just feels outdated. Played 'til the end it's overlong, and there's almost always a runaway win. Also there's a dumb hallmark of "classic" games: an "anyone on this space moves up 3!" spot that we always ignore. It'd probably be better just to play until a certain number of points or something. Usually I'd just suggest ditching the point system altogether (as I do with Concept), but since this game is actually about tricking people the points feel appropriate.

RATING: 9.5
What a hidden gem. This has never disappointed and is always hilarious. Tip: chuck the board and response slips. Pull cards and give everyone a looseleaf sheet; having everyone play as normal, writing responses on normal paper. I love this game because it goes through four distinct phases:
1. Discovering that the craziest, battiest saying is ALWAYS the correct saying, because people from ancient times were very very weird.
2. One of the players 'gets' the game and throws down an answer so nonsensical that it couldn't possibly be made up, except it is and you actually got someone to believe that "The stalk that stands straight...blows southerly"
3. One of the players writes something from modern times, like "Clay cooking pots...are at least better than Tupperware." Nobody votes for it, but everyone laughs.
4. Someone writes something too modern to be the real answer and everyone still votes for it because it's so funny.
After this point, there's a great mix of all four phases: too-crazy real answers, crazy player-generated answers, and great funny answers. The game can go as long as you need it to and generate some great memories. "A Spaniard who sings...is mad or has no money." Classic.

#2 on personal list 'Top 10 hidden gems in tabletop gaming'

RATING: 7.5
This is the game the 'Balderdash' wanted to be.  With Balderdash, sometimes there's just not enough to go on to sink your teeth into defining a word, but with the beginings of phrases in Wise and Otherwise you always have a solid place to start, and it's much easier to be convincing with your endings than it is to be with made-up definitions that seem to exist in a vacuum.

The actual old phrases used here are often much funnier as well.  "Even if the tree snake is long, he can't... sleep on two anthills at once."  Really?  Who knew?

An intelligent group will make this game even more fun, but it's often a good laugh to just throw out some garbage line.  Our games have included stories of mad geese chasing little brothers and the classic phrase "Blah blah blah, [Swedish stuff] blah."  Which so accurately described burn-out from a late game night, that we all got a kick out of it.
