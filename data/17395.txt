RATING: 5
Preliminary rating after playing the Leuthen battle scenario from [i]Prussia's Glory[/i], which is the same system. Interesting if cumbersome combat system. The abstractions are a little strange. It's clear that modifiers are much more important than odds in combat.

With regards to modifiers in combat I think the designer was trying to emphasize the importance of leadership and unit quality over raw numbers, but an analysis of the CRT may leave you baffled, mainly because the CRT doesn't make a lot of sense until you get into the "7 or greater" range. The net effect is that good die rolling appears to trump good tactics to a large degree.

The other abstraction that's strange is the "supported defense" rule where it actually becomes more difficult to attack two or more units in the same hex if you're flanking them. In some cases it works out that you're better off [b]not[/b] flanking those units, because then only the top unit is counted for defense. It's stranger still that facing doesn't matter.

The result is a game that doesn't feel particularly tactical, with abstractions that seem far too outlandish to be enjoyable.

Traded.

RATING: 5
Well, after many years I finally managed to progress to the second game in the series.   This was quite a barrier for me as I'm not fond of the counters or the "blob" style command rules with their rather abstract state changes ("activated" or "degraded" are not terms you find in any military manual), and the convoluted and sometimes strange army morale system.  However, deciding battles by army morale is still a good choice in principle.  The choice of including the approach march is still brilliant, and GMT finally chickened up to include the originally planned fog-of-war rules.  Unfortunately at this point the people who'd have been willing to play this ftf have moved on to other games.  Presentation still marred by counters that look beautiful close up but give no feel for "linear warfare" when deployed, and  maps without the roads that figure prominently in the battle accounts.  However, kudos to the designer for sticking to his vision and putting those battles out.

OK, after playing a number of games so I sort of have the fiddliness down I could see myself returning to it if I have the chance to play a full scenario with fog of war. So I have increased the rating just on the strength of that feature.

The view of the battlefield from 10,000 feet. Don't be fooled by the superficial resemblance to other GMT titles like GBoH, Glory, or Musket and Pike. This is not a tactical game, it's grand tactical or quasi-operational. The game wants you to ask "What on earth was Frederick thinking attacking uphill at Kolin instead of going around to the left," not "What would have happened if the 57th IR had gotten off a few extra volleys." 

There's a lot of grinding and die rolling. The system assumes you are willing to do a hefty amount of work to visualize what's happening inside the hexes. Some may conclude it's entirely too much work.

RATING: 5
A nice try on the wonderful topic of Frederick's battles.

I like some of the ideas. On the most positive side is the need to use your cavalry wisely. If you charge too early you will be disordered and an easy target for your opponent. If you charge against non-disordered infantry, you waste your cavalry. That is good.

On the downside: The system is a very fiddly. There is too much going (ruleswise) in one hex. Like others have commented: the scale is not good. There is no feeling for linear warfare. Instead all action takes place in a 3x3 hex radius, with dumplings of counters pounding each other. I am not satisfied by this.

The BAR series gives a much better feel for this, and tells a much better battle narrative. However, it takes much more time to play.

A good, large scale (500 m), moderately complex tactical game.   Is not an up close, linear formation game, scale is too big for that. Lower complexity allows for play in an evening.  Its strengths are it allows for pre-battle movement that historically occurred in SYW.  Another strength is it mostly puts you in the role of the overall commander in large part by limiting the use of less reliable formations, and Command initiative abilities. But does a surprisingly good job, considering the scale, of implementing smaller scale features by use of stacking and factors affecting battle resolution, such as cavalry vs. infantry.  Rating may go up a little.
