RATING: 10
A heavy box loaded with fine materials: good quality miniatures, plenty of deluxe modular map tiles, character’s record sheet, rule book, adventure books (for the Kickstarter edition), a great variety of tokens, colorful markers, fine designed coins and a multitude of event, gear, exploration and even magic cards. 

The game manages to merge pirate life with fantasy in an adventure full of events and surprises. Encountering Royal marines and conquistador skeletons to cannibals and monsters. It can be played solo or co-op. Lots of different characters can join your pirate group, each with a variety of abilities and talents. The whole story is comprised by 10 scenarios that when linked together they form a piracy saga dripping with theme. Each player keeps a written sheet with all his achievements and misfortunes (loosing a leg or a hand), his prestige and booty. Although being a team each one tries to become captain at the same time and lead the team. 

Game mechanics work well, rules although easy, one keeps turning pages to find that particular subject or item missing or for a clarification. Nevertheless once played you grasp the rules and game momentum flows with fun.

Each scenario consists of the adventure phase where your team of pirates tries to achieve the goal of the scenario by eradicating enemies on modular map tiles and a voyage phase encountering sea monsters, merchant ships and coast guard brigantines. The scenario ends by reaching your port on Tortuga island. There it is time for rest, wages, recruitment, provisions and acquiring new skills and weapons. 

There are so many characters to choose from, such a variety of decisions, modular boards, events and situations that makes every play unique and enhances replayabilty. Probably one of the finest pirate board games in the market.

RATING: 7
After the slog that is the rulebook for the 2 major phases of the game there's actually an enjoyable game here. A lot of questions came up when playing the game that made it very hard to enjoy the game at first as the rulebook was unclear (probably due to bad translations).

The pirate theme is really good and you're constantly trying to one up other players to gain all the glory for yourself. You get to fight over loot, prestige, and being the ship captain. The adventure and voyage phase are great and make for an interesting dynamic.

The one thing that seems really clunky is the enemy AI. A lot of times since enemies spawn when you reveal a tile, if you happen to retreat back the enemies just tend to pile up on top of each other and walk in single file to their death or block each other from ranged combat so it's easy to game the system and just have them walk into a buzzsaw of death as you wait for them to approach through a narrow passage.

RATING: 6.75
Fun Pirate themed dungeon crawl.  Campaign became a bit of a slog (so much so that I didn't even finish the last mission).  Melee Combat was similar to Mice and Mystics where you roll/defend before the enemy attacks you and you defend. Until someone rolls really well or poorly. Ranged combat was roll to hit with no defense so definitely shoot first and ask questions later.

The voyage phase was a bit of a mess and the port phase was a too-light version of the town phase in Shadows of Brimstone.

The miniatures are soft plastic and nearly all of them needed straightening.  Cards were okay with a small amount of equipment variation.  Some of the tokens were a hard to read as there was too much going on.  The plastic money coins are fun and add a nice feel of collecting treasure. 

I'm holding on to this to see if Skull Tales 2.0 (2018?) "fixes" some of the major issues.
