RATING: 9
On no planet is this a "board game", but it is an elegant indoor (or indeed outdoor) play-anywhere translation of Boules using small, heavy beanbags. Perhaps the best part is its flexibility and variety -- the jack can be thrown anywhere (onto stairs, on a shelf, in a tree) and whoever placed it that round can set conditions like "only throw with your non-dominant hand" or "throw with your eyes closed". There are also rules for trick throws like landing on top of another beanbag to compensate for the fact that they are so much easier to aim.

Very simple, very accessible, very good.

Ratings history: 8 -> 9. After more plays it really does get even better. Get creative with the conditions -- throw onto or off a moving platform. Balls must be rolled, not thrown. Throw them out of sight down or up the stairs. Throw past obstacles. Throw onto a tile/paved grid and only balls on the lines between tiles score. There's a huge amount of replayability in it and you can take it anywhere.
