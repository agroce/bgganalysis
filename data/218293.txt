RATING: 6
I was really excited to get this game but I found the game play somewhat mediocre.  Certainly not a bad game and it is well produced with great art; it's just that I think there are better worker/dice placement games out there.  I thought the concept of reputation (dictating the sum of the dice values you could place each round) was intriguing.  It's just that it really isn't integrated into the worker placement actions all that well.  I also believe the game play is too complex and long for the level of strategic depth in the game.  I wouldn't use this game to introduce new players to worker placement.  I also wouldn't play this game with people well versed with worker placement.  So, I'm not quite sure the situation that I would get this to the table again.  That said if somebody dropped it in front of me, I would play it.

RATING: 8
I had a very good first impression of this game. It has a lot of things I like: worker placement (it's in fact dice placement), with a clever mechanism (the reputation track) on how many you can place, collecting resources whom you use for collecting nobility and VP.
But how good I found my first game, I'm wondering how good replayability will be? There seems to be a logic you have to follow each game: improve your reputation so you can place more or dice of higher value, first you'll have to invest in settlers and then you can switch to the 'task'-tiles, which makes you'll have to increase your nobility at least until the first level also during the 'settlers'-phase.
There are however a good dose of expansions already in the box, so hopefully they'll provide the necessary variation a game needs.

RATING: 7.5
A couple of plays in with 2 and then 4 players. For the price (KS deluxe edition) decent board, artwork and components. Fiddly mechanics, all paths to points come with layering that can be a lot for new players to digest (wanna house your settlers? Great, but first you have to get the resources you need, but you can't play enough dice yet to get them, you need to raise you reputation first, it would be nice to get some resourcing / money bounsing too via the guilds, but you need to place workers on the guilds to do that, and to do that you have to...house settlers, oh and don't forget to buy another house too, but wait, you don't have enough money). All this being said, once you do get the mechanics figured it is an enjoyable game, but it could turn off lighter players. The 25 minutes per person is questionable, even as we really began to figure things out, with more people at the table things really slow down in the later half of the game. Don't think I'd want to play this with more than 4 at most. It could use a better way of keeping 'settled' cards from 'cards to be settled,' as the table got crowded (and this one is table monster) some players began to get the two mixed up.
