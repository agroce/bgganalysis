RATING: 3
Undoubtedly a game like Tempel des Schreckens depends a lot on the group playing it and, as undoubtedly, the group I played it with wasn’t ideally suited for it to succeed: anyhow, the game itself didn’t add anything to our experience to improve that feeling of a poorly designed game that loomed over us each and every time.

On the surface, it looks like a very loud game and it actually is, making for a lot of bantering at the table (which is good, very good!) but it also gives the players too little tools to discover the other players’ identities: the only way to check if one is bluffing or saying the truth is by group-flipping all of his chamber cards in a single turn, which might seriously jeopardize the adventurers’ chances at success though (and surely bores the uninvolved players to death).
Moreover, there’s no way to force a player to reveal how many treasures he actually has, which under the right circumstances might make guessing where they are a pure act of faith. 

Even if each player’s identity would be clear, there’s absolutely nothing two players on the same team (say the adventurers) can do to help each other besides stating: «Either card in front of me is the last treasure we’re hunting for; the other, the third trap that loses us the game. Now, good luck choosing the right card».

As much as I love randomness in my games, Temple des Schreckens is way too random: actually, it is a textbook example of a game playing by itself. And one of the worst hidden identities games I’ve ever played.

Das ist auch bei TEMPEL DES SCHRECKENS, einer Alternative zu WERWOLF nicht anders. Allerdings kann bei TDS keiner rausfliegen. Das Spielidee ist ein einfaches Team-vs-Team-Spiel: die einen sind die Schatzsucher, die nach Schätzen suchen, die anderen die Tempelwächter, die eben genau dies verhindern wollen. Der Titel ist eine schöne Reminiszenz an Indiana Jones und teuer ist es auch nicht. Es ist aber kein Kartenspiel, wie man im Spielwarenladen irrtümlich glauben könnte, obwohl es nur Karten hat. Das schreibe ich zur Warnung an alle, die  Omas und Opas, die ihren Enkeln etwas zum Geburtdstschenken möchten.

Jeder weiss also was er hat, aber nicht wo! So hat auch jeder eine Chance zu manipulieren etc. Denn falls man frühzeitig herausfindet wer der böse Spieler ist, wird dieser nicht mehr dran genommen. Die Regelnänderung macht es spannender.

RATING: 8
[b]Tempel des Schrekens[/b]

[COLOR=#FF0000]Initial thoughts and rating (8/10):[/COLOR]

[i]I was happy to finally get a chance to play Tempel des Schreckens which is a very easy to learn bluffing game. My two plays have been with eight players, but I think the game would scale very well based on how the roles are distributed and no one at the table knows anyone else's identity. The tension builds as the game plays since you can never be certain who is on your side, roles are never revealed and each side has ample time to bluff their way to victory. Both games we played there were guardians that most of us had pegged as adventurers which lead to some really exciting moments; we split our games, one for the guardians and one for the adventurers. 

The rules are a breeze to learn and teach making it accessible to just about anyone; we'll definitely be playing this with our families over the holidays. The game is also the perfect length, a full game of five rounds took less than 20 minutes. 

It needs to be said that just reading the rules or watching a video will not do this game justice. You really need to play it to understand just how well designed and fun it is. While Cockroach Poker is my favorite bluffing game, Tempel des Schreckens is close behind and if you add Ca$h N' Guns to the mix you have the perfect trilogy of bluffing games. I really appreciate games that can create such engaging gameplay experiences with simple rulesets. 

*Just an FYI, I had to import my copy from Amazon.de, but it was definitely worth it.[/i]
