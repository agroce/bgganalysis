RATING: 8
Similar in concept to Zombicide games, but offers a unique theme, much more variety and tenser atmosphere. Many different weapons, items and events make this one more interesting. Combo mechanics, skill trees, allies, various enemy types, portals and sabotage elements through pact cards take it even further.
It has many tense moments where you have to make risky decisions  (going 'all in' with a difficult combo, betting everything on getting through a portal to safety, sacrificing your health for a dash through a window to still have one activation to kill a demon in order to avoid his attack, etc.), but  can also be very swingy with situations where the board can be overrun and appear hopeless. At the same time, if you get the right weapons to the right characters you get a chance to mow through the hordes. At least until you get betrayed by a resurrected accomplice... 
The amount of various elements and the different elements to consider also make it a bit more fiddly than the classic Zombicide games and the rulebook doesn't do a very good job of teaching that, which may lead to frustration. Once you're familiar enough with the round order and enemy types it should go smoothly though. I definitely recommend checking out the FAQs as well (especially keep in mind that combo damage is not split up between enemies!). Also make sure that you shuffle in some randomly selected weapons among the first item cards, or it can be a frustrating start.
It's quite tough and not the most streamlined experience, but once you don't spend so much time anymore looking up enemy abilities and managing game mechanics it flows well enough to get immersed in a hellish trip of dice chucking, fear and mistrust (which is always a good combination).

Sinceramente parece que los creadores tenían muy buenos ilustradores, buenos diseñadores de miniaturas y una imagen muy clara en la cabeza. Pero que a la hora de materializarlo, han hecho un amasijo de reglas y no lo han jugado para ver lo que han "cocinado"...

PD: Sorprendido de la nota general y muy curioso que las mejores notas sea de jugadores españoles... Lo siento, pero no puedo creer que alguien diga que este juego tiene un reglamento fantástico... Efectivamente tiene una apariencia genial, con ideas de maquetación muy guapas, pero que a nivel de redactado de reglas es pésimo. Falta resumen del turno para agilizar la partida, en las cartas no sabes si los efectos son de un solo uso o no (habiendo espacio de sobra en las cartas), muchas lagunas que entorpecen la sensación de juego...
