RATING: 7.5
This game is extremely dry, it's ridiculously over-complex by modern standards, and it takes far too long to play. But I love it so. I've never played anything like it. The different factions are extremely different from each other, and even within the same faction the various ship classes and specs lead to a staggering variety of gameplay. For example, Klingons have highly maneuverable ships, which leads to an exquisite dance of weapon arcs, speed, and vulnerability. By comparison the Federation's "great mace in the face" photon torpedo armed ships are like bulls desperately trying to line up a killing gore on the smaller wolves that harass them. And yet it is the Klingons, not the Feds, who actually field a full-on battleship, seemingly turning the tables in those engagements where it is present. When you add the submariner Romulans, live-fast-die-young Orions, noble Hydrans, Kzinti, Lyrans, Tholians, Andromedans, and others it means that every player can have a favorite that is extremely different from their friends' and yet balanced. The game includes boarding parties, "hero" caliber crewmen, fleet operations, as many different scenarios as you can imagine, and a timeline that spans practically from Armstrong to Kirk and beyond.

I still have a good 8' of shelf space devoted to this game, and yet I wonder if I'll be able to play it. Perhaps it was a perfect game for my college and post-college years, when I had more time and gamer friends than I had obligations. Maybe I can convince one of my buddies to run a scenario with me. Just a nice, simple duel...

RATING: 10
My personal favorite game of all time.  Most of the basics have been covered in other reviews or articles, so my comments I'll limit to opinion.  The *reason* I like the game so much is both in how perfectly balanced it is, and how well thought out the universe is.  No plot holes in the history of the SFU (*cough*like franchise Trek*cough*), no technobabble explanations for things or 'technology of the week' devices that only show up once.  The technology balance of the races is perfectly level, and the assigned "point value" of the ships allowing pick-up games is accurate (IE., matched fleets of similar ships equal in point value WILL be a balanced game).  The game is internally consistent, balanced, and FUN.  Most impressively, the game doesn't feel "gimmicky" - the technology present works in a logical way consistent with the universe.  Transporters, for example.  No evil clones or curing diseases or restoring aging or any such nonsense.  These are warships, the transporters can beam over troops to enemy ships to try and take them over, or for hit-and-run raids with explosives to take out key systems.  They can beam "mines" into space, and can be used for moving cargo.
