We played Germans vs Russia but instead of feeling being the upper hand as Germans I really felt I was the defending party. Wasn't expecting that. It took me a lot of scheming to reach my goals and my opponent was feeling way to confident with all the troops he could bring into battle vs only a few of mine (due to high cost units and a very difficult position to get more points to support my army). So it was fun, but not what I was expecting. There is a good game hidden under the scenario's but didn't have a really balanced feeling. Dice play a big roll and although I understand that there is a change of absolute failure, it's still strange to be on the losing site and win in one strike.   

So need more gameplay. Why a 9? I think it's a good game, haven't seen te tip of the iceberg.

RATING: 8
Preordered early bird on 13 July 2016.  Have to wait how long? +10 for one Play Mat set during the survey. Estimated delivery: March 2017 Actual delivery: 1 June 2017, 3 months late.

2GM Tactics is a card wargame.  In terms of mechanics, it is very similar to other card game which has a fixed playing area made up of 8 x 6 tile terrains, e.g. Kard Combat which is about use of magic.  You can see the designer's wealth of experience in a  a multitude of other proven wargame basics and integration of these basics in a new model and medium (i.e. cards!).  The game also reminds me of the Spearpoint 1943, also a WWII card wargame design.  Thanks to the more well-structured sequence of play like other wargame design, 2GM Tactics works more in a more sensible manner than Spearpoint 1943.  For example, the Movement segment is done before Deployment (a concept which is quite different from other "normal" wargame design) so that there is a need for the players to plan ahead where the upcoming deployment should land on the edge of the map.     

The players can "construct" his own deck based on the sides, the Army, and limited amount of points, 120 points in a classic engagement of Battle Royale, with the objective to destroy enemy Headquarter (which has 10 life points).  The "points" of an Army sounds a lot like those miniature wargames and the "draft" echoes card games.  The cards are rated for firepower, weapon range, damage, movement, defense value, life-points, etc. Some special cards confer advantages to the gameplay, e.g. ammo to give more damage, command to give more supply, airborne a unit, etc.    Attacks are probability based - you roll a 10-sided die and hit on a equal or higher number than that.  Critical hit can also be scored if the number is high enough for each specific weapon.

The game can be completed either quickly in 30 minutes or dragged on for 20+ turns for two hours, depending on the skills of players in drafting the deck, ability to play the cards organically, and ability to plan and foresee in the medium term how the game is going to develop.  Replayability is high as there are numerous expansions and thus cards to mix and match.  To me, the shining point of the game, and you know what, is how the game tackles the supply problem.

Supply Points are needed to bring and deploy the units from your hand.  The hand size limit is 7 cards at the end of turn.  Otherwise, you have to discard the excess.  Supply Points available in a turn depends on the "length" of your supply line, which is extended from your base in terms of the number of tiles to the frontline.  The further it is, the more Supply Points coming.  For example, if you can have at least one unit in any tile on every row for three rows consecutively, you will get three Supply Points.  The base always provides one.  Command unit can give more Supply Points depending on its location as well (up to three maximum).  Any "interruption", i.e. absence of a friendly unit along the horizontal row of eight tiles, would break the "supply line".  It makes sense to protect your supply line so that you can secure the much needed Supply Points flowing without pause.  

Overall, the game excels my expectation of what a WWII card wargame can offer.  By far, it is the best one to my experience.  Certainly, it is better than Spearpoint 1943.  2GM Tactics is designed in Spanish and the English edition is released through Kickstarter campaign in 2017. I haven't got deeper into the various historical campaigns and scenarios, deck-construction, etc.  But they look very promising.

A 1-4 player tactical skirmish game played entirely with cards and little cardboard chits.

Although the underlying mechanics are decent, this game is really brought down by poor translations across the rulebook and especially on many of the cards.

Beyond the glaring translation issues, the game also suffers from an avalanching leader problem where winning the first exchange or two will nearly always result in winning the game, although it takes forever to actually go through the motions. The problem is bad enough that even starting with an inferior hand can honestly lose you the game.

As a final negative, those little aforementioned cardboard chits can be awfully fiddly for a game that really plays quite simply at its core.

For all the problems this game has, I almost regretted making the decision to part with it. Almost...

El teniente Winters fue conciso respecto a aquel villorrio: “Haceos con él o dejadlo en ruinas”. Nunca hubiésemos pensado que los kartoffeln jugarían tan cruento papel en aquella incursión…
El 506 de la aerotransportada sirvió de avanzadilla en el campo de batalla, y una docena de bravos paracaidistas cayó como una bandada de halcones para defender la casa que quedaba más alejada de nuestro destacamento de infantería. No habían hecho más que confirmar por radio que ya estaban con las Thompson apostados, cuando nos advirtieron de que la cadena de un SdKfz  251 arañaba la calzada del pueblo.
El grupo de mando no dudó por un momento de que había que moverse, y sin mayor dilación, nos dividimos para tomar posición.
—Alfa-Bravo, a las “once” —nos indicó el alférez señalando la vivienda más occidental—, Charlie-Delta, a las “doce”. Nosotros tomaremos la “una”. ¡Martin, Swanson, Duval: quedaos en el Bofors, no quiero ni un Alcaudón revoloteando por el cielo…! ¡Qué Dios nos bendiga!
Cuando mi tropa accedió a la casa, oímos la explosión de lo que parecía una bomba lapa: el 506 había acabado con el Hanomag pero los muchachos advirtieron de que tanto ellos como el edificio estaban muy perjudicados. La comunicación se cortó después del rugido de las Machinenpistole.
El grupo de mando apenas tuvo tiempo de pedir apoyo de artillería al estado mayor, pues, desde la linde septentrional de la aldea, las salvas de un Flak 36 y de un SFH18 hicieron saltar por los aires su edificio y el adyacente al nuestro. 
Parecía que de poco iba a servir la aparición de un Sherman por la calle que ascendía hasta nuestra posición pero de improviso, la casa que habían defendido con uñas y dientes nuestros paracaidistas, estalló en llamas. Buenos chicos los del 506. 
Se conoce que los artilleros alemanes debieron entrar en pánico porque huyeron hacia el norte, no sin antes haber dejado el pueblo asolado a excepción de la casa en la que nos habíamos parapetado. Eso, o que se habían agotado los turnos de la impresionante partida de 2GM Tactics que acabábamos de disputar…
Mucho se perdió en aquel altozano pero al menos no consiguieron hacerse con él los nazis.
Juegazo, juegazo. Enhorabuena a los creadores por esta joya. Muy recomendable.
