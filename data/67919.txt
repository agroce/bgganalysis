RATING: 9
I own 30+ board games and The Message is definitely my favorite card game by far. The theme of the Chinese version is actually based on a historical period before the outbreak of the Chinese Civil War between KMT and CPC, where the undercover agents of the two parties fought secretly over intelligence. It's a popular theme for historical novels in China, and an award-winning movie "The Message" is a successful adaptation of one of the novels. The game is sort of a spinoff of the movie. 

I have owned all three versions of the game (original, Emissary Crisis, and the Blacklist) and I have to say I like the original version the most, which, unfortunately, is out of print now. The Blacklist made some changes to the illustration and the abilities of the characters. The original illustrations were more accurate in the sense of cultural representations, while the new ones are either too western-superhero-like or Japanese-anime-trynna-be. And the revised abilities are less balanced and it shortens the game time which compromises the joy of planning carefully on each move and the experience overall. But I really like the new action cards and the two-colored intelligence cards added to the sequel. I like the concepts behind them and they add more variability to the game. The Emissary Crisis is actually very well balanced and everything, and it has a more international theme to cater for non-Chinese players. But as a Chinese, I relate more with the original theme, and that is the only reason why I like the original version more than the Emissary Crisis. 

It's a pity that the language (and maybe poor marketing, too) limits its influence worldwide since it is such a well-designed game, especially considering that the board game industry in China is not that developed and there's little originality in modern Chinese board games. The Message is really a masterpiece I'd say.
