RATING: 8
A very unique set collection/card drafting game.  The mechanisms are quite interesting.  When you draft your card from the "market", the card that gets revealed is the card that will trigger a scoring for that specific type of card if you have that type of card already layed out in front of you.  The sacrificing of produce cards is also unique and creates a competition to get the different produce cards worth more points.  It's unlike any other card game.  And it's not heavy.  Fun and recommended!

RATING: 7
Early impressions: Obtained this due to good ratings from Geek Buddies. I was not disappointed. Game play has some similarities to Byzanz and other stock market games where ultimate value of any stock depends on your contribution to the common pool to determine value of the stock but in the process, thinning your own available stock. I enjoyed the process of evaluating the stock but didn't like the semi-hidden aspect of the game.  Can be easily rectified by  placing all face up stocks in their respective columns while keeping some of the hidden information hidden on the altar.

I quite enjoyed the in-game scoring that is triggered by the last card that was not taken but perhaps not as thrilled by the luck of the draw when a new row is refreshed for scoring. That said, this is a minor issue. That said, I do not like the Oracle variant which places even more "luck of the draw" in the new column that was refreshed. This is going to tempt players to refresh the card columns hoping for a lucky Oracle draw.

The game play is quick and can be completed in 30 minutes. This is a great gem and likely going to be in our collection for the foreseeable future. Glad that Rapa Nui was finally reprinted.
