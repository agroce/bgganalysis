RATING: 8
The game is in the baseball hall of fame for a reason.  The elegance and simplicity of this game are amazing.  It plays fast and logical.  It caputures extreme nuances of baseball, such as the lefty-vs-righty match-ups and the range of a shortstop versus his accuracy (error).  The two are not necessarily one in the same: you can have a guy who never makes an error, but can't cover much ground (thus turning potential outs into base hits).  And the game is played on player cards with only a few charts, and it plays in about 30 minutes.  It's probably the most perfect sports game ever made - especially in terms of realism vs. fun factor, however, I rate the basketball game as the 2nd best game overall of all games.  The only reason I give it an 8 is that I am not as much of a baseball fan as I am a basketball fan.

RATING: 10
I played APBA for years and it's a great game. It's shortfall IMO is the Basic game is too basic and the Master game to too complex. Constant pitching vs. individual batter lefty/righty calculations and constant base-running vs. fielder throwing calculations. So APBA Master is too much "work" to be fun for me, so APBA has no happy medium.
On the other hand, I've only recently have gotten Strat and it's awesome!  Three levels of play, Basic, Advanced, and Super Advanced.  Basic it too basic for me, but is great for guys who want to crank out games in 20 minutes.  Advanced is definitely that happy medium for its accurate lefty/righty splits, and excellent fielding mechanic. But I quickly stepped up to Super Advanced and don't think I'll ever look back.  It's simply fantastic fun for this solo player. The extra fielding realism, more detailed base stealing mechanics and added realism of individula ballpark effects are just right for me.  Not so much "work" doing math problems.  And the Super Advanced only adds maybe 5 more minutes to each game.   
APBA will always be a favorite, but Strat is definitely my favorite tabletop baseball sim.
