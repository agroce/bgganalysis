RATING: 5
The rock-paper-scissors mechanism is what it is, and the dice make this game quite luck-prone, which for the length of the game I guess is ok. Where the skill lies is covering for as many probabilistic eventualities as you can, which is probably the best you can do given the die rolls. Some maneuvering space is reserved by the ability to use 1-3 dice. Still, this is a tactical game with a few heuristics thrown in for good measure. This red-green-blue RPS makes my head hurt though, so not sure how enjoyable this would be in the long run. Will give it another shot, but don't have high hopes for it beyond filler level..

+ Interesting mix of dice and RPS
+ Portable and quick to break out
+ Great art

= Too much luck for my taste, it seems

- I found the red-beats-blue-beats-green tough to remember, perhaps more sessions are needed to internalize

Players are trying to move their ninjas from one village to the other by competing with other ninjas. Which ninjas move is governed by rolling 3 dice (each one corresponds to the paper, rock, scissors).

RATING: 7
We liked this a lot. Very simple game play and I lost twice in a row. But I was taking more risks and attacking, and I failed, partly due to luck. It looks fantastic, it plays very quickly and we enjoyed the challenge. Definitely one to keep playing.

I was very struck at how different the flow was to say a Knizia game. We're used to 2P games being a face to face attack, or even a tug-of-war. But this game of walking passed each other, seeking out the hidden village, that really felt different. I might be projecting Eurocentric views, but I do feel this is different in approach and feel to a western design philosophy.
