RATING: 8.4
This 9.0 is provisional as I consider the CoH series as one of the better wargames out there. But this system really shines in its SOLO play. Nothing in tactical wargaming land comes even close (except the old Ambush).

Although the solo module for Guadalcanal is not available (yet), you can play both sides with the solo card system of Awakening the Bear (or use the excellent solo chit system) found on these pages of BGG.

That being said: I think the solo play is a bit hampered in Guadalcanal as the vast majority of scenarios uses hidden set up which makes solo play cumbersome. 

Conclusion: Start the series with CoH Awakening the Bear, PLUS buy the East Front solo expansion of this box. It REALLY is the best in tactical solo play you can find on the market. Then you can buy the other expansions, but by all means use the tactical solo cards (or spend chits found on BGG) to play the games in 2/3/4 player mode or 2 seated solo mode.

It is the only system where pure infantry, artillery and armor/tank units is so well and EASELY integrated.

RATING: 8
I'm generally not a great fan of tactical level wargames, but Guadalcanal is a really excellent game, certainly far better than Combat Commander, which I had quite high hopes for, but turned out (in my opinion) to be slow, fiddly and altogether unsatisfying. Guadalcanal, on the other hand, moves along at a rip-roaring pace, with real tension in the decision-making. The rules are presented in a logical order and are in most cases very easy to understand, particularly if you've already played some wargames. The quality of the components is excellent, with the board artwork a real bonus, although it's not always easy to see the hex edges. I expect my rating will only increase as I play more scenarios.
