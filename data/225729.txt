RATING: 9
This is a solo game I'll happily recommend. I get a Onirim vibe when I play this. It's a fun and interesting puzzle and you have to adapt to the cards that come out and their order. During the game, cases get solved, the deck gets thinner and difficulty increases and you realize you shouldn't have discarded that key card you now desperately need.
It's got beautiful dark artwork made by the designer but probably not recommended for children. Bastwood's comment on the artwork is spot on... [i]I have a hard time describing most board games set in Cthulhu mythos as "Lovecraftian". Most of the time the games leave nothing to the imagination, with vivid illustrations of the Old Ones flailing their tentacles about in every card & miniature. That's why I find Arkham Noir so refreshing. The top notch illustrations on the cards evoke an ominous and dark atmosphere while explicitly showing very little. Something seems to be wrong in each image, but you frequently can't pin down exactly what that is. The game lets you conjure up the worst horrors instead of slapping you in the face with them.[/i]
