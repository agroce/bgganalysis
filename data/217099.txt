Demoed it at Essen 2017. We managed to play only 2 rounds but we got the basic feeling of the game at it looks nice! It revolves around deckbuilding. Each round you draft cards then there is a cool mechanic of using your ambassadors to collect cards from the board. It requires a smart placement of discs to have a majority of your color cubes on given card. And even if you did poorly it's not that bad because you can promote more discs and you will surly do better next time. Then you play cards and build your little engine but there is a lot of interaction with other players. It looks like a solid and fun but at the same time demanding game. The theme is there somewhere but we found it very loosely connected to the game itself. 
I would gladly play the full game.

RATING: 7
Complex tableau building game consisting of three main action phases: drafting new cards, bidding for additional cards in a system known first from Ornella's Hermagor and finally playing new cards to your tableau and executing effects. Additionally, points are scored each round. So far a design with everything but the kitchen sink.

The core of the game is the playing of the cards to the tableau. There are dozens of individual cards, complex effects and combos, targeted player interaction. Enough to dig into, probably too much unless a group dedicates itself to playing this game repeatedly.  Otherwise, the potential won't be revealed. From the artwork and the title I would have expected something in the vein of Eclipse but it's more Euro-ey. Supposedly, this is the first of ten designs in the Seeders series. I wonder if the game's appeal is strong enough to escape from the niche it's in and provide the base for realizing the whole series. The ambitious price for what essentially is a massively blown-up card game leaves doubts.
