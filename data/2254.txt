RATING: 2
There is hardly any game here -- the only decision point is to decide on one of six strategies at the beginning of a round, and you don't even get to do that in every round (also, some boxers can go into "Cover Up" mode during a round).  The strategies simply apply some modifiers to card results.  Beyond that, the gameplay consists entirely of flipping the next action card and looking up the results.

On the plus side, over 400 individual boxer cards are included, many with bios on the back.  Interesting stuff for people into this genre of stat-based sports sims. (Personally, when it comes to sports sims, I'd rather play a video game.)  I sometimes pull this off the shelf just to read through the cards, even though I have little interest in actually playing the game.

I'm a boxing fan. If you aren't you probably don't need this game. If you do, it's a statistically based game that let's you choose a tactical approach round by round, like a manager might do in the corner, and unlike in the real world, your boxer will always at least attempt to follow instructions. Beyond that, you play the cards and observe what happens.

There is a wealth of statistical information in the cards, with a very large number of boxers represented, and on the whole I'd say the stats are well done. As with any such game, the joy comes in allowing you to vicariously enjoy re-living classic matchups, or creating ones across generations to explore might-have-beens.
