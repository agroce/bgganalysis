The clue-givers bid on the number of words that they can accomplish the feat, with the lowest bid getting the honor of doing so. If the team cannot successfully name the words in the allotted time and with the bid number of words (or less), then the other team gets the point. The first team to collect ten points is the winner.

RATING: 5.5
Game in a Nutshell: Bid on the number of words you'll be able to use to get your team to guess five words. Pass-or-play!

Played as: 8,12 player.

Pros: Simple social party game with some direct interaction with the other team while bidding on who gets to play and then the moment of truth when you see if you can get your team to guess the words. It's fun to try and imagine your optimal words while bidding and compare it what your opponent uses on the times you pass.

Cons: The down time is considerable since it takes 30 seconds or so to play your words, and only holds real interest half the time when your team gets to play. Besides that, the regularity of how often you get to enjoy the bidding may only be a 2-3 times a game which is the real slow-down point as all but two players bide their time waiting for however long it takes the two active players to analyze and bid, re-analyze and so on.

Other: I guess party games in general are supposed to have down-time in order to socialize, but I've never considered myself a party-gamer so take my criticism with that in mind.  I think this would be a really interesting team game with just 4 people. That should minimize downtime considerably and make the game much more intimate and personal as the funnest part (the bidding) is more frequent, and you'd have a higher sense of camaraderie with your team-mate as there's only one person to play off of.

Best Use: You only have four people and still feel like a party game.
