RATING: 7
A run-of-the-mill co-op zombie game with an interesting twist: some players may secretly get bitten when attacked by a zombie. These infected players will cause their fellow uninfected players to lose if everyone manages to escape. The suspicion and uncertainty that grows as the game continues is really the heart of the game, and makes this a ton of fun to play. Friends inevitably betray friends who may or may not have been bitten. Infected players attack healthy ones to dispel suspicions. No one trusts anyone. It's an awesome and very thematic idea.

The game is not perfect, however. Rules for zombie spawning are so confusing and weird that even the designer tells you in the rulebook that they don't make any sense and to just "do your best and have fun" while playing. Not a good sign. These spawning rules will plague you on every single player's turn, so it's crucial that a coherent ruleset be put in its place. Hopefully if they release a version in the U.S. these problems will be fixed.

UPDATE: I've been told by the designer that they will be Kickstarting this in the U.S. and that zombie rules will be clarified in the new edition. (which will also have minis!)

Initial Rating: 7.0 (July 2013)

What a surprise. I love Zombie games, but I wasn’t expecting much from this game, however I really liked it. IMO Huida de Silver City beats Zombicide in every way except in the game components department. I think it will go up in my ratings after more plays.

I love the theme, the gameplay is great (you have different possible paths to salvation, you can become a zombie or and infected adding to the paranoia, NEAT, the characters are very well done and the inventory system is innovative (for a boardgame). You really feel the tension each turn. 

On the bad side, the rules of "zombie spawn" appear a bit confusing and moving a lot of zombies each turn can become tedious. 

Bottom line, a very good game. You really feel the theme and stress on this one, one of the best zombie games I have played. I want to buy it.

2016-01-27

In my mind, I really liked Huida de Silver City. I liked it so much that I bought the game from Spain at a high cost. However when I played it again, I didn’t enjoy it as much as before. I think it has to do a lot with my current gaming tastes. I have changed a lot during this past year and I have drifted away from Ameritrash games.

Another issue I have with the game are the components. They were really hard to un-punch, it took me literally hours to do this and still some of the tokens got damaged. I had to cut some of them with a blade. Huida de Silver City has the second worst punch sheets I have ever seen (the worst are. by far, Exodus: Proxima Centauri). When this task ended I was very angry with the game. The inventory doesn´t match what is written on the box.

I’m not sure if I will keep the game in my game collection, but I will give it (at least) one more chance because I love the theme and I have fond memories of my first play.

Huida de Silver City is no longer among my favorite games of 2013.
