The mechanics are interestingly laid out, giving some incentive to follow, some to mutiny, and being the Captain isn't always the most desirable position (especially if people keep stealing from you).

RATING: 3
I'd be interested in learning why this game was published, or put another way, why the publisher thought it publishing-worthy. (And to make it worse it was actually crowdfunded, meaning that peope paid good money to have this go to print.) It is a run of the mill action selection game (not worker placement, for you only have one worker) with only one and two-thirds of a selectable action per turn. There is very little blocking or priorisation save for, perhaps, the conversion step of the two resources into money and VP. The game goes on and on and on and on, and is just a featureless grey twilight of collecting sufficient resources until it is worthwhile to convert them, and repeat the process. If you've played three or four rounds, you've played them all. The game reminds me in an unpleasant way of Drum Roll, which is just as basic and uninteresting. Really, look for a different game. You know, one with an actual [i]game[/i].
