RATING: 6
[url=https://www.boardgamegeek.com/geeklist/210126/item/4685760]1 play, 3er[/url]

This is a simple little abstract (with a medieval theme of sorts). You put together a board, made of a bunch of double-sided pie wedges. Each wedge has a different action. Players have a set of 5 pieces -- we placed these randomly during setup but you could take turns and be more deliberate. On your turn, you can first opt to bribe first (spend 1-4 coins for a special power). Then move one of your pieces clockwise, exactly 1 wedge per piece on its starting wedge. When it lands, you do something depending on the character moved (place a banner on the wedge if you have majority of pieces there; steal money; get money; activate a different character; etc.). The first player to place all their banners (1 on each wedge) wins immediately.

Interesting, but I'm not a fan of abstract games and this felt very abstract to me.
