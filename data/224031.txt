RATING: 5
(+) Simple and cleanly accessible ruleset and accommodated a higher end player count.  
(+) Interesting shared interest in that you are kept engaged by what spaces your opponents are positioned on, and wary of your own spaces that may provide opportunities.  
(+) Crunchy decisions present as a result of the above point. You have a meaningful choice in how to spend your movements across your pirates each turn. For such a simple ruleset, this level of depth, however minimal, is a pleasant surprise.  

(-) Extremely subject to the luck of the draw, which can become frustrating if your luck is poor.  
(-) Can become repetitive with fewer players and less dynamic position changes.  
(-) Play feels scripted. Players are positioning strategically early game, and once someone decides to make a "break" for the exit, everyone rushes to the end. You can mitigate this by good planning and and play, however.

RATING: 4
Note that I haven't played any of the older versions so I'm not sure how they compare.

Not a bad game. The push-and-pull between moving back to draw cards and moving your opponents forward to draw cards can be fun.

However, the luck of the draw is really significant. Sometimes you could shoot forward with several of your pirates if you only had the right cards, but you just don't have them. I've also seen somebody with three pirates in the middle of the board pull out a win because they just happened to have the right cards to move all three to the end.

This brings up another issue, though: bad play can hand someone else the game. If someone ends their turn leaving an easy path to the end, and the next player happens to have the right cards, they get a huge boost or they can even get an outright win if it's closer to the end of the game at that point.

For the length and lightness of the game, I don't consider these problems to be too big. It's basically a filler that shouldn't take more than 30 minutes at any player count. However, there are many other light games I would rather play.
