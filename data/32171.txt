RATING: 8.5
British Meetings in the Victorian Era. It's a narrative game, in-between a boardgame and a role-playing game: acting is needed to add flavour to the game. Mainly, it's a deduction game which kind of reminds me of Zendo on steroids; the players try to solve the enigma with yes-no questions which can be pretty straight-forward or more complex than the web of Arachne, and all that in only 1-hour. There are interruptions (in a "take your daughter to the slaughter" or "once upon a time" fashion, but more controlled) and time flies surprisingly as the enigma unfolds (or gets more complex like a cat's cradle).
