RATING: 8
Step aside Dutch Blitz and Ligretto - Nerts has far more to offer!

I had been thinking about getting a copy of Dutch Blitz for a long time. A friend from college had one, and I really enjoyed the game. Then I learned about Ligretto, it's more colorful, less provincial cousin from Europe... All of Ligretto's splash and style came at a cost - a literal cost - usually 150% ~ 200% the cost of Dutch Blitz...

But before I pulled the trigger on the one-trick pony of the farmland game, I discovered Nerts.... Nerts, which is played with *normal playing cards*! Nerts, which doesn't limit itself to one game - but actually can play *all the games*! Every playing card game you can think of - from Poker to Rummy, from Old Maid to Crazy 8s, from Solitaire to 52-card pick up... it's all here! AND you can play Nerts, the open-source, public domain, free-to-all, ethnic-neutral version of Dutch Blitz / Ligretto.

Not only that - but having these matched decks of playing cards, that only differ on the color of their cardbacks, mean you can, if you are so inclined, use them to play games like Scan or Indiscretion, where the backs of the cards tell you want suit the card is. Just pick 4 of the 6 supplied colors and get one suit from each color and play the game.

There are so many possibilities here, it was a no-brainer. The cards are nice, not quite Bicycle-brand quality, you won't use these for any special magic tricks - but they are decent enough. I'm very happy with my purchase, and I think you will be too.
