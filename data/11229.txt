RATING: 6
My criterion for rating games


http://boardgamegeek.com/geeklist/147053/item/2300390#item2300390


1) Sense of wonder: The wide variety of maps, scenarios from the books, and types of squads make this game play out quite differently each time. Also the theme and looks really bring you into the game. A little samey as far as the gameplay though. Rush up, fight, end game

2) Meaningful/fun decisions. Great decision opportunities throughout the game from the making of your squad, to troop deployment, to strategy within the game.

3) Player interaction:   Combat, and lots of tactical movement. This game has a TON of interaction.

4) Components/artwork:  Fantastic miniatures and the artwork is nice.

5) Game length/complexity:  Length of the game is ok, but with building squad, pulling minis out, and the wide variety of special abilities this one can be a little tough to teach and to play with newbs. Takes a little too long to setup. If you come with prebuilt squads it is better, but this requires a dedicated gaming group.


Bottom line: This is a great miniatures combat game. I always have a good time playing it. I haven't played in a while and I need to get it back to the table.

RATING: 7
OK, the obvious downside is the collectable distribution of the game- it sucks here just like it does in every other collectable game but the good news is that this is a fast, fun miniatures game that dispenses with a lot of the nonsense and bookkeeping that plagued the D&D mini system.  The rules are solid- detailed but not overly so and there are provisions that make even a lowly ewok or stormtrooper a viable squad pick. The result is a completely accessible game that probably has better "gateway game" clout with nongamers than any silly game involving train routes or anything to do with renaissance trading.  I think the game derives its strength primarily from its license- indeed, if you're a Star Wars fan you'll be thrilled to see your favorite characters pop out of the boosters and you'll be tickled at some of the bizarro and hilarious matchups that inevitably happen- like IG-88 vs. Princess Leia or that Tusken Radier vs. Wampa fight you've always imagined.  The game takes to scenario play extremely well but 100pt multiplayer games are a real treat- we call it "Star Wars Beatdown".  Looking forward to the space battle component!

RATING: 7
A collectible skirmish game based on Wizards of the Coast's D20 combat system, featuring a wealth of characters and vehicles from nearly every era of the Star Wars universe. The quality of the character models have varied with each release, going from truly awful in the Clone Wars set to excellently sculpted in Bounty Hunters. The stat cards have also gone through a few iterations, but they have always been pretty bland anyway, especially when compared to the rest of the game's components.

The skirmish rules themselves do an excellent job of creating battles that feel right for the Star Wars universe. Most games start with a ranged shoot-out and end with a close-up clash of lightsabers. The cover rules can be a little confusing for new players, but they add significant depth to the system and are largely responsible for the game's authentic feel. I only wish it scaled better to higher point games. The massive ground battles often featured in the movies are largely unplayable with this rules set. Also, there are a handful of severe imbalances in a few character abilities, and playing against them in opposing squads can be frustrating. This is easily avoided in casual play, but hurts the game's tournament appeal. 

My biggest complaint about Star Wars Miniatures is its cost. The game is extremely hard to collect due to the inordinately large amount of ultra-rare figures, usually 16-18 per set. Collecting a complete set requires a perfect pull from the purchase of FOUR CASES of figures at about $150.00 a pop (and that's assuming a discounted price). In recent years, booster prices have crept up to a MSRP of $14.99 and $22.99 for the Huge figure expansions. RATING: 6
[b]I BENT MY WOOKIE![/b]

I'd like to bless my comments with another phrase or two about the "power of the dark side", "the force", or "these are the figures you want", etc, but they've all been done to death by every other commentator on the Geek. What a ruddy shame!

Like the stunningly good D&D miniatures, these Star Wars models are so good by themselves that you do not need to use the D20ish system that they suggest you play with. I won't.

What is absolutley freakin' marvellous about these little pieces of moulded plastic, is that you can act out any scenario in the SW universe, just like you did as a snotty lad, roll dice, make up any rules system you want, and have a bloody good time doing it, because all the characters are catered for; with the possible exception of the Space Slug, Garbage monster, etc. The figures are about 28mm  - the BEST scale - and are nicely painted, mostly, with great sculpting.

Q: Who wants to play with a couple of AT-STs and blast a forest chock-full of nasty little Ewoks?
Q: Who dreams of recreating the Hoth battle?
Q: Who'd like to move an army of battle droids about a dining-room table, ruled over by Grievous, Jedi Hunter?
Answer: ME!
All these scenes, and more, can be expanded by the use of this range.

As with D&D, these models are sorted by rarity into blind boxes. Genius marketing. I'd do it too. You know you would as well. I don't want to collect them all - which would be madness. I just want to craft memorable Star Wars scenarios.

You may get some bent models (Battle Droids seem to be the worst), but that issue is solvable with the old hot'n'cold trick.

My favourite miniatures: [i]Droideka, Jango Fett, Rancor, Cody, and any clone trooper or battle droid.[/i]
