RATING: 5
My thing about two-player abstract games is that most of them have enough going on to keep me interested, and Linja is no exception.  There's something neat happening with the way that you move the pieces forward on the board, and there's a timing element in when you choose to push forward, but it also feels like it reaches a point where the game becomes a bit determinate.  I've only played it once, so I could be wrong, but it was like we reached a point in the game where it was clear that one player was going to win, and the other player couldn't do anything to change that.  I prefer games where the push-and-pull lasts a bit longer, and Linja doesn't have that characteristic.

1 play: 2 players
