[i]Posted: 10/23/12, Rated: 6[/i]

[i]Canasta Caliente[/i] is apparently a variant of [i][thing=17104][/thing][/i] -- shocking, I know.  It is a Rummy variation using partners, where the value of your initial meld increases as your team crosses scoring thresholds.  So what makes this game so "caliente?"  As far as I can tell, it is having the point values of cards clearly marked; a "Stop" card (although there may be a similar provision for "Stop" in regular [i][thing=17104][/thing][/i]), and some 100-point bonus cards.

All of these frills result in a game that feels like scoring is chaotic.  It makes sense, eventually, but wild point swings are very possible.  As with any [i][thing=15878][/thing][/i] variant, control of the discard deck is vitally important.  Often times the first team to seize the discard deck ends up winning that round.  The other trick is to manage your hand size: it is tempting to play all of your cards as you go.  However, if you get down to having only one or two cards, then you can't play a set after you draw and still have something to discard.

My wife and I have friends who love to play this game, and they consistently beat us.  Another annoying property of this game is that I just can't seem to get better at it.  I'm sure this frustration is reflected in my rating, but ultimately I would choose a different game to play if possible.

Game in a Nutshell: Draw and discard your way to collect sets of cards. Get more sets then your opponent then go out by getting rid of all your cards.

Played as: 2 player

Best use: Playing one-on-one when the person's company is more important then the game being played.

1 partial play (just 1 hand, 3 newbies).

A nice rummy variant: partnership (sit across from partner), 1 meld area/team, play with 2 normal decks and 4 jokers shuffled together, may only meld sets (not runs), random bonus 100VP if you're dealt the right card (silly random rule like the flowers in Mhing), wild cards worth 20 or 50VP (wilds may be used as part of meld but in initial meld the majority must be non-wild), need at least one canasta (meld of 6 or 7 cards) to go out, cards are worth 5 / 10 / 20 VP, first meld must be worth at least a specific VP (higher requirements as your score gets higher -- catch up mechanism), can pick up the entire discard pile if you can use the top card immediately in a new meld, get negative VP for cards left in hand if someone goes out, special STOP cards prevent next player from taking discard pile (discarded wilds also act as stop cards, as well as "freeze" the discard pile: to take the pile, you must make a wild-free meld with the top card). Play to 5000 VP.

Hard to evaluate after just one hand, but it seems like this could be a lot of fun! As a learner, I'd much rather play with this deck vs. regular cards (so I don't have to remember 2s are little wilds [20VP], jokers are big wilds [50VP], 3s are ... stops?, etc.). However, the game's been around so long that I'm sure people who've been playing it a while will prefer the more basic cards.

You can also play with an extra rule, but we didn't play it and I don't remember what it is!
