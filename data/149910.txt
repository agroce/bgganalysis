Szabály rém egyszerű:
- vagy leteszünk egy korongot egy üres helyre
- vagy a már lent lévővel lépünk (de csak másik korongra, üres helyre nem)

1 korong - gyalog
2 korong - futó
3 korong - ló

5 korong - királynő

Az nyer, aki összehoz előbb egy hatos tornyot (király). Kb. ennyi.

RATING: 10
If you look up 'clever' in the dictionary you will find a picture of Six MaKING next to it. The mechanic of building towards a winning stack of 6 or more (called a King) using the different movement abilities of the chess pieces based on stack height is quite ingenious (1=pawn*, 2=rook, 3=knight, 4=bishop, 5=queen). But the really interesting part of the design (and trickiest to visualize) is the fact that a stack can be split in the course of its movement, making for many resultant combinations between starting stack and ending stack. I really like and appreciate Boardspace.net optionally superimposing the image of the chess piece that each stack is able to move like. It really helps with being able to visualize moves, which is especially useful for beginners. In fact, I like this idea so much that I cobbled together my own set using a 5x5 wooden Siam board, enough Connect-4 interlocking checkers, and several sets of square, flat, diagram chits that fit perfectly into the concave part of the checkers. This is a must for any fan of 'simple but deep' abstract games. I need to try the championship mode sometime -- the winner being the first to create 6 Kings, with each King being redistributed among the players as soon as it's formed.
