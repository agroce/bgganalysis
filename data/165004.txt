I prefer ONUS! over Battleground Fantasy Warfare on those points:

ONUS! is in spanish and comes with printed rules in color.

ONUS! has slightly better art and graphic design. 

ONUS! uses cardboard chits to keep track of unit information. No more writing cards with erasable markers.

All unit costs in ONUS! are rounded off to the nearest ten, so building army lists requires little calculation.

ONUS! has a smaller but manageable scale. Less armies, less tables, less rules and less units per battle. The ancient battle theme requires less chrome and overhead (no flying units, no giant monsters, no magic blasts...). But still offers a lot of variety.


However I still prefer Battleground Fantasy Warfare over ONUS! on those points:

Units in ONUS! have a beige colored background. They don't look good on a green playing mat.

ONUS! has less assimetrical play, as its armies don't have big strengths and weaknesses.

ONUS! lacks the mechanical uniqueness Battleground has. Battleground is the only miniatures game I know in which all your troops have a permanent AI behaviour that you may change or ignore during your turns using command points.
