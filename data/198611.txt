RATING: 7.25
For a Dexterity game with a deal of randomness, this one has a lot going on.

Mondrian asks players to use a variety of methods to deliver dice to a grid of Mondrian-esque blocks of color. Players' dice can interact, and the numbers on the dice matter as well as where they end up.

At the end of each round cards are awarded to highest set of dice if the number of pips is >= to the Point value of the card- but players can add to their pips by sacrificing cards from hand to make up that difference as well.

At the end of 4 rounds, players assemble like-sized blocks of cards (paintings), and compare scores based on card values, and on bonuses for using largest numbers of same-colored blocks.

Because higher valued cards tend to be single color squares, where low valued cards are more likely to have several blocks of varying color, the assembly of the painting can be an interesting exercise as well- and remembering the number of blocks of color captured by opponents is difficult.

All of this makes for somewhat zany, light fun.  It's not a game where you will remember every decision and think about them, but a game where every turn of the die may well affect one's choices- and it doesn't overstay it's welcome.
