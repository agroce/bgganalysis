About [thing=125048][/thing]:

[b]1) What is it?[/b]
[thing=125048][/thing] is a demo version of the game [thing=63268][/thing], containing two mini games and 16 simplified (in the full game the cards have more symbols) cards to play.

The game uses speed and pattern recognition, and is played in real time - usually in a great hurry.

Is playable and thaught in less than 5 minutes.

[b]2) How do you play?[/b]
There are two mini games, both easy as one can be:

Game 1) Reveal two cards. The first person to name the symbol that both cards share, take both cards. Once the draw deck ends, the person with the most cards will be the winner.

Game 2) Each player holds one card and everyone reveals them at the same time. Then, when a person name a symbol shared in her card in one of the opponents, she gives her card to that person - if the player has more than 1 card (having already received one or more from others) she will pass all the cards she has. The round ends when only one person has all the cards. The game will end when the draw deck is empty, then the person with the least cards will be the winner.

[b]3) Which are the decisions made during play?[/b]
None. Just find the symbols fast and talk quickly.

[b]4) What are the good things in the game?[/b]
- Fast, being also easy to teach and to play;
- Small and extremely portable;
- Language independant;
- No learning curve - even small kids can play evenly matched with adults;
- It's free.

[b]5) Which are the bad news?[/b]
- No real decisions;
- Real time isn't something everyone likes, as it adds some stress and frustration to those that fail a lot;
- Small replay value (it is just a promo, after all).

[b]6) How do you feel while playing?[/b]
Like playing a competitive match of Whack-A-Mole - you need to be fast and precise. It isn't a game of politely waiting or for those AP prone.

[thing=125048][/thing] does a good job in giving the flavor of the game in a small promo package.
