RATING: 6
I picked this game up, several years ago, strictly on a nostalgic fancy.  I remember playing this with the grown-ups in my family and having a really good time.  Considering these memories were from the early eighties, I should not have been surprised at the disapointment that I felt after playing a few games.  There was a single year in which this game was manufactured, which should indicate how successful the game was at merging video games with board games.  

Gameplay involves moving from area to area within one of four territories.  Players input this data into the tower, which generates an encounter and displays it by spinning, and lighting a single image on a transparent cylinder within the tower's window.  This continues until a player has found all three keys and can enter the tower and attempt to win the "Final Battle".  There is no player interaction.  Tracking data is done on a player peg-board.  Needless to say, with all the different types of dungeon-crawlers and adventure games out there, this is underwhelming and feels slightly automated.  The only decisions available to the player is where to move and when to go to the tower.

I don't have any regrets with my purchase.  The game looks good when setup and it helps me remember fun times as a child.  I'll definitely open it up and play it with my daughter when she's old enough.

+1 Personal Rating for nostalgia.

If you don't like this game, you take gaming far too seriously.  DO NOT play this with the droid app.  Playing this game without the tower is like buying a Ferrari and storing it in a glass garage.
Some say it's all luck and no strategy, but these statements are not completely true. There are some elements of resource management and movement planning.  The best strategy is to get around the board as quickly as possible but it's easy to get distracted by the desire to have more troops or buy goodies from the bazaar.  Granted, if you can't stand games with dice rolls or elements of luck then you won't like how this game (or life, for that matter) treats you.  The fun is in the interaction with the tower's old-school midi sounds.  The gameplay itself is reasonably easy for most kids and non-gamers to learn but there is some strategy once you know how the game tower 'thinks'.  My goal is to simply get a rating by the tower of something other than 00!
