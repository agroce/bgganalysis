Like most of the Munchkin series, this game is a fun, simple game to play with casual gamers.  Lots of alliances and back-stabbing between players and certainly a good 'ice-breaker' game.  Whilst there is an element of strategy in Munchkin, a lot relies upon the vagaries of the cards which means that the eventual winner is often decided by a chaotic scramble. 

Munchkin games really only work with more than three players as that really allows the social dynamics of the game to come out.  The rules are simple to teach but I feel that the game lacks a depth that will leave most board game enthusiasts cold.
