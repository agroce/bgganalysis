RATING: 5
I'm really surprised at the high scores this game has, a lot of which seem to be ratings from people who hadn't even received their copy yet (as of 9/10/2017). My brother in law kickstarted this, and we played it yesterday. It's a great concept, but in practice is just... fiddly. Our second game, as we'd tried one of the starter maps first, was made using the random map rules where we built it ourselves from our party of 4. But some of the areas were simply so complex to get too that there was essentially no way to access the oxygen there without two people's characters working together to do it. Had they chosen to fight for it, we would have sat there for an eternity, and in fact we just chose to call the game and assign those oxygen points to whom we thought most likely to have received them. Perhaps the game is better using the premade maps and with experienced players, but the ease of making 'unwinnable maps' was kind of a turnoff. Also, the component quality is not good- the cardstock is decently heavy, but the print job is TERRIBLE. Everything is way, way off center, to the point there was no way they'd not have noticed during a check before okaying the print run- so they either just didn't care, or didn't check. Some of the tiles even have most of the cog wheel sides cut off, to the point you can only tell they're supposed to be cogs because they're a different color. It's disappointing because the game concept is really cool, but we found playing it to actually be pretty fiddly- I'd stick with the premade maps perhaps.
