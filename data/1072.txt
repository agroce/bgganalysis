RATING: 8
What sets this game apart is the requirement of "drawing" with a partner, alternating laying sticks/stones.  Inevitably one partner will look at the other in disbelief, wondering why the partner put that particular stick/stone there.  It's very interesting to see how differently people can envision something, something also evident in a game like Monstermaler.  This game would take a little work for someone that doesn't speak French, since the players must be familiar with the objects on each card.  I worry a little bit about replayability, since there are not that many cards, and the more difficult ones really are quite difficult, though I suppose you could always just make up your own words.

RATING: 9
Awesome Pictionary-esque party game. Unique in that there are no teams, yet there is a lot of team work. This means that all players are actively involved; you don't have to simply watch an opposing team take its turn. 

In Pictionary/Charades, it's amusing to see a player draw/gesture in frustration. Double that with a team unable to communicate with each other!

Unfortunately, it's only available in French (where I live), which would be a disaster if I were to play it with my family. Consequently, I'll probably improvise my own version with a deck of words recycled from one of my older Pictionary-esque clones from the 90s.
