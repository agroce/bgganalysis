As "nothing but auction after auction" games go, this is pretty good. Not my favourite genre, but the minimum prices (high for the good cards) means you can only afford to buy a card each round or so, so the auctions go fast. Some of the action cards can be results-changing and kingmakery, going for top dollar, and when and if they come out can make or break you, especially if it's the turn you were planning to "go out". As the game is nothing but choosing which cards to go for, liking the game will include accepting the randomness of which cards come out when and doing the best you can with what comes when.

RATING: 5
This game is very much like its predecessor. The slight rules tweak, new artwork and abandonment of card collection can't really improve it. For a start, there's a repetitively dull multi auction mechanic that's just not interesting enough to drive this mini beast. (And I usually like auction games.) It's also too open with too many cards on the table and too chaotic to allow the players to really let themselves get carried away and have fun. I don't often think this in games, but there's too much choice and not enough structure here. It reminds me a bit of Illuminati without all the arithmetic, but also without the multiplayer interaction on a turn. Nice try the first time around, but not enough has been done to resurrect this one and really get the full potential out of all those lovely cards. Pity because there's a good game in there somewhere waiting to get out. Maybe the next time around?
