RATING: 7
The worthy successor to Russian Campaign, and the baseline "one map" WWII strat/operational west front invasion game.

Graphics -- counters were excellent for the time, and still solid 30 years later; map was/is... not real pretty, but does the job, and is the weakest part of the game.  Another gloss white glare-fest ala Nap War and Peace of the same era; easily remedied with some light green marker or other light wash.  

Gameplay is cluttered on the map, long with weekly turns, and it's a generally tough slog for the German side, getting pounded turn after turn by allied air, but the CRT and some good unit differentiation make it varied and fulfilling, and it usually matches the history.     

All in all, I haven't played it much, but when I make the effort to embark on it, it scratches the itch every time.

RATING: 8
A great game of the western campaign WWII beginning with Normandy. Unfortunately it took me a couple decades of shelving it and playing only a few turns before I could play an entire game and it also took me a couple decades to learn to like it. The hardest thing to overcome for me was I couldn't accept the game system's mandatory combat rule for units adjacent to enemy, and the double impulse thing.
Coming from lower echelon games (Firepower, Arab Israeli Wars, the two Panzer games) and higher echelon games (Rise and Decline of Third Reich in its new release days), I found it difficult to accept the game mechanics of Fortress Europa.
What it doesn't do very well is replicate grand strategic decision-making. Neither does it replicate well combat at the lower tactical level.
What it does extremely well is replicate decision-making, trials, tribulations, objectives and concerns of Operational level campaigning. The Supply and support aspects are very abstracted through the use of Headquarters counters; yet very "real" in that FE's supply rules are extremely deadly and WILL force both side to husband, protect, and keep in reach of those HQ counters every turn, all through their campaign.
Now I love FE, would like to play it all the time and on Vassal if anyone wants to give it a go let me know!

RATING: 4
My second ever wargame.  I have always found this game a bit too fiddly, and therefore uninspiring.

Unlike The Russian Campaign - with its grand ebbs and flows and The African Campaign - with its smaller scale, but perhaps more dramatic swings of fortune, Fortress Europa involves a steady build up on one side and decline on the other.  In other games (like Storm Over Arnhem) I have found that a delightful way of building tension for both sides.  But in this I have found it more hard work than anything.

My feelings about the game are relatively ill-informed:  it has been many years since I played.  But faced with a choice between three similar systems, this was always my third choice, even back in the late 1970s and early 1980s when I only had three choices.

I spent a lot of time playing this back in the day. It was one of the first AH games with double-sided counters and they were quite handsome, although the board art was boring and also the maps stopped too far West in Germany (I made up my own board to reach Berlin).

The mechanics were good overall, although I was never a fan of the compulsory attacks - there was always a one-hex no man's land between the two sides.

The OBs were at divisional level with some major non-div units. Stacking always penalised these and the selection of which non-divs were portrayed is hard to understand - for example no artillery or combat engineer units are shown. This would make it hard to optimise a force eg for a river crossing or to attack the Westwall.

Overall though it was a manageable and plausible game. 

The AH General did a good update of the (very abstracted) air war which gave both sides some leeway as the use of their strategic air forces.
