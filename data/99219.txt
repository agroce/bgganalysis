RATING: 3
I like the game Set by the same company so I expected to like this one too. I didn't, for 2 reasons:

1) Visual spacial recognition talent is far more important that skill acquired by practice. So a person with this talent will always win against a person lacking in this talent, no matter how much the talentless person practices. For persons lacking this talent, the game may seem like homework, not fun.

2) Large swishes are far more difficult than small 2 or 3 card swishes. But the scoring doesn't reflect that. Related to this, the rules are unclear regarding whether a 4 card swish that can be decomposed into 2 two card swishes is actually a valid swish (for details on this issues, see: http://boardgames.stackexchange.com/questions/7837/are-2-2-card-swishes-a-valid-4-card-swish).

Set is a similar type of game by the same company that doesn't have defects. So there's no reason to get this game instead of Set, though I suppose if you're in love with Set and want a little variety, this could provide it.

RATING: 7.75
An abstract card game that uses spatial skills to match circles and dots on transparent cards. It's an injustice to compare it to "Spot It!...it does have a similar matching/speed element yet it invokes way more brain burning if trying to match a lot of cards. You can take the "speedy/easy" path of only picking 2 matching cards, or you can take the risk of trying to match 3, 4, 5, or even 12 cards to maximize your score. It's an inexpensive and brainy filler with visual appeal that entices people to try it out. Since I don't have many abstract card games this one stays in the collection.
