RATING: 6
Warband fits what I am looking for in my collection. It reminds me of a Martin Wallace design, Euro with theme. Also, it fits my interest in an army fighting together but generals battling each other for glory. 

It is easy to teach and can finish within an 60 to 90 minutes (3 players or less) which makes it a great Meetup game. The interaction is also very good as there is no way one person can do everything and everybody has to piggyback on every other players actions. In addition, I bought Darkmoon and Warbands and Warbands was far superior in quality compared to the professional company produced Darkmoon.

Downsides is the game can be mean (which I don't mind) but that can be reduced by a change of deck composition a bit and if one wants to optimize, AP can be a issue as you have to wait for your turn to maximize decision making due to the results of the other players actions. Thus this also means that at 5 players, the game can be overly long and reason why I prefer it at max 4 players. 

Overall to me I am happy with the purchase which is always a good thing.

RATING: 2
FIRST IMPRESSION:

This game just made me angry. The longer I played it the more I got angrier and frustrated. I don't like giving really low ratings, particularly due to the touching story on the back page of the rule book but this one is a memory I wish to forget. 

What I did not like:

1. Rules describing how casualties from conflict worked were poorly written. Thankfully, the example given was very thorough and helped us work through that confusing aspect of conflict. (The rest of the game was relatively straightforward but casualties was a bit complicated). 

2. Powers of the races do NOT feel balanced. Part of this plays into which races are in play and may pile on well from another player's ability. My halfling's extra dollar when I taxed was about the weakest power in the game. My opponent's abilities saved them several dollars each turn in promotions or gave them free troops when someone promoted themselves which was a frequent occurrence. Ridiculous.

3. Starting player feels strong out of the gate. They get to attack relatively cheaply if the early players get out a lot of captains. Forces the rest of us later order players to pay more money to fight (and to rub it in, the money gets given to the opponents since they likely have a lot of captains out!).  When one sees the first two players only have to pay $1-2 early to go to war but then have to pay $6-7 when it is one's own first turn AND give that money to the opponents with the head start, my blood was boiling. 

4. The intel cards ran out. Guess when the ran out. They ran out after the first two players in turn order in the final round. Damn turn order. Intel cards can give a ton of points (12) if you can acquire 3-4 of them and place 3-4 scouts but it was pretty much luck of the draw. Sure, you draw 2 and keep 1 but I wasn't lucky in my draws while the two who tied for the win got more. 

Enough - I could probably rage on for a while, as I'm still frustrated with this one several hours later but I think I got some of my frustrations across. :-) I don't know if it is broken, but there is no way I will ever play this game again willingly.

RATING: 7
Interesting game. It has a fighting theme, and it's not a co-op game, but you don't actually fight the other players. It's a euro game with multiple paths to victory.

I wish they could have somehow streamlined and used some different terminology for the Fight action. Overall the game is very simple, but this one action has 9 steps and each player gets a double-sided reference that just explains this one thing. After doing it a few times it starts to make sense, but it seems like it could have been handled better. For instance, there is a part during the fight where you can send cubes to the medic, and there is a separate part where you take casualties. Sending to the medic and taking casualties sound like the same thing but they're handled differently in the game.

There's also a valid criticism that the art and icons on the enemies could have been handled better. The cards all show the same few icons and don't show any actual enemies, so you never really feel like you're fighting. You just feel like you're counting up icons and comparing them. As a euro gamer this isn't really a problem, but nice art would help get more people into the game.

Other than that, the gameplay is pretty smooth and plays pretty quickly. I'll play again when asked, but I won't be grabbing for this because of the minor hiccups.

Curious fantasy euro game of up to five races that viciously despise one another yet find themselves working together to save their home world from the coming darkness. But it isn't cooperative. Your race wants to be the best at it. Each race has unique strengths. Improvable player actions, upgradable worker cubes. Fight and defeat darkness enemy cards before they pillage regions. Place scouts for intel. Compete with other races for control of Captains. Force other races to take casualties. Also competing for area control of regions you have have majority of intel cards. A lot to manage. I love the tie breaker for installing a new Captain...the ousted Captain chooses.
