RATING: 4
I did not try Road to Leningrad before this one.

Hmmmm... pretty much structured around the East Front Series with a lot of rules similarities and some differences, like the chit-pull system and choice of Assault, Mobile operations and CRT.

Unfortunately, I was seriously put-off by the gaming proposition. There are a lot of rules, exceptions, things to remember, a lot of details, steps, modifiers, cross-references... too much. I would have streamlined the whole thing considerably.

The strategies/tactics are quite obvious and I did not find that the options were satisfying, choosing whether to conduct an Assault or a Mobile operation is usually a no brainer.

Ergonomics are poor with considerable stacks with markers etc. Once I realized that most of my pieces were on the KG/BG holding boxes I wondered if the scale was right.

All in all I found that this was a lot of work for relatively little fun. If it had been simplified, somewhat cleaned up of unnecessary minutiae, it would perhaps be a keeper.

RATING: 9
One more thoroughly researched battlefield developed into a boardgame. The contents are of fine quality; the trademark of GMT. The rules are extensive and detailed on every aspect of these engagements. They are so well presented and in such cohesion that there will be no need to go back and forth searching for that particular exception in the mechanics of play. For most part of the play the German has the initiative and the power to breakthrough Soviet lines; always finding gaps and opportunities. While the Soviet player is in defense there are always chances to counterattack and snatch Victory Points from his opponent. 

All factors and parameters have been taken in consideration to adapt the exact environment of that era in the wargame: weather, supply, air units, armor, artillery, strongpoints etc. For each factor there are comprehensive rules to be implemented. The game includes a couple of scenarios for each operational region (Mtsensk & Mozhaysk). The time frame for each scenario can reach up to 7 days of fighting and every game turn is almost half a day. The Combat Results Table has been designed to conform with this time scale and provides realistic outcomes. Do not expect to command large maneuvers. Although the blitzkrieg theory is put in practice at every opportunity the historical facts impede major achievements and encirclements due to bad weather, terrain and supply restrictions. 

The chit-pull system assures replayability and the type of operations chosen (Mobile or Assault) presents players the harsh reality of warfare. The game has grasped the real situation and historical factors of these military operations.

RATING: 9
My first von Borries game and definitely not my last.  I really enjoy the scale of the battle and the counter density, for me, is perfect.  Thanks to the chit-pull mechanism, the game plays great as a solo experience.  While some have noted that the rules are a bit fiddly, I find that the logic behind them makes a lot of sense once you start moving units around.  Sometimes you miss some key points, like when I discovered that Artillery/Anti-Tank/Anti-Air units cannot advance after combat, but overall I find the various mechanisms to work together pretty smoothly.  It's also a lot of fun to have an East Front game where the Soviets are not just automatic pushovers.  You will definitely take a lot of losses as the Soviet player, but use of defensive terrain and timely counter-attacks really demonstrate how 'brittle' the German forces can be.  Would love to see more games in this series.
