RATING: 6
Smarty Party is a trivia game that does a pretty good job of solving the "either you know it or you don't" problem of most trivia games.  Each question has a list of several valid answers, and players offer answers in turn until all are guessed or until a prescribed number of wrong answers are given.  You are penalized for wrong answers and rewarded if you are the last one to come up with a valid answer.  The genius of this system is the way that the answers given will progress from more commonly known items to more obscure items.  All the players will feel like they're a part of things, whether they know a little about the category or a lot.  Unless of course they know absolutely nothing about a category, in which case they might feel colossally stupid.

Unfortunately, the quality and consistency of the lists chosen is disappointing.  First of all, there are too many pop culture lists, in which you either know a valid answer or you don't.  I wish the designers had focused on lists that allow for educated guessing, such as "heaviest land mammals" or "top world religions by number of followers."  Secondly, the question set is steeply skewed in favor of male players.  Third, there aren't nearly enough cards included for a trivia game such as this.    Still, it's a very good game.  I'm hoping for an expansion with more and better lists.  Meanwhile, I might make a project of making up some of my own for others to use. [Update - the expansion sets have much better questions on the whole.]

RATING: 8
Granted it is a party game, but I think it is a very good one that keeps everyone involved until the end.  I don't tend to like obscure trivia games (e.g., Trivial Pursuit) but I still want some thought and strategy to go with my party games (such as in Times Up).  Smarty Party accomplishes both admirably.  Sure you can sit there and just give the first answer that pops into your head but you can be more strategic.  For instance, to play well you need to figure out what the least obvious answer is and then reserve that to get the bonus as the last one to get a correct answer.  Also there is the questioner's forecast of whether the others will score above a certain number of points, so everyone is involved in every round.  I also like that the person in last place is the questioner and their position on the board is protected from deteriorating further (and may improve if they forecast correctly).  This keeps everybody in the game until the end which makes it more interesting and fun, particularly for non-gamers.  All in all this is a great game that you should absolutely try if you like party games at all.
